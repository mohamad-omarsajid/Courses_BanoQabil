# 3.3 Retouching

!!! info "Why this lesson?"
    By the end of this lesson you can clean up small marks in a photo, copy good
    parts over bad parts, fix light and shadow, and work in a safe way that you
    can undo later.

    *(Roman Urdu: Is sabaq ke baad tum photo ke chhote daag saaf kar sako ge,
    acche hisse ko kharab hisse par copy kar sako ge, light aur shadow theek kar
    sako ge, aur aise tareeqe se kaam karo ge jise baad mein undo kar sako.)*

## What you will learn

- What retouching means
- The Spot Healing Brush
- The Clone Stamp
- Dodge and Burn
- Why you should work non-destructively

## What is retouching?

**Retouching** (Roman Urdu: photo ke chhote masle theek karna jaise daag hatana)
means fixing small problems in a photo. You remove a spot, clean a mark, or fix
uneven light. The goal is a cleaner photo that still looks real.

Good retouching is gentle. You are not making a new face. You are just tidying up.

## The Spot Healing Brush

The **Spot Healing Brush** (Roman Urdu: brush jo ek chhota daag click se gayab
kar deta hai) removes small marks fast. You click on a spot, and Photoshop fills
it with skin or color from right around it.

Use it for:

- A pimple on skin
- A dust spot on a wall
- A small mark you do not want

Pick the tool, set the brush a little bigger than the spot, and click once. The
mark blends away. It works best on small marks with plain area around them.

## The Clone Stamp

The **Clone Stamp** (Roman Urdu: tool jo ek jagah ki copy uthata aur doosri jagah
lagata hai) copies one part of a photo onto another part. You pick a clean source
point, then paint that copy somewhere else.

Here are the steps:

1. Pick the Clone Stamp tool.
2. Hold **Alt** and click a clean area. This sets the source.
3. Let go of Alt.
4. Paint over the part you want to fix. The clean copy paints in.

Use the Clone Stamp for bigger areas, like removing a wire or a person in the
background. It gives you more control than the Spot Healing Brush.

!!! example "Remove a background object"
    Say there is a trash can behind your friend. Pick a clean patch of wall as
    your source with Alt. Then paint the wall over the trash can. The can
    disappears, and the wall looks normal.

## Dodge and Burn

Two old photo terms control light.

- **Dodge** (Roman Urdu: ek hissa thora roshan karna) makes an area lighter.
- **Burn** (Roman Urdu: ek hissa thora dark karna) makes an area darker.

You brush over a spot to lighten or darken it. Use Dodge to brighten dark eyes.
Use Burn to bring back a bright sky. Go slow and gentle, or it looks fake.

## Work non-destructively

This is the big rule of editing.

To work **non-destructively** (Roman Urdu: aise kaam karna ke asal photo kharab
na ho aur tum sab kuch undo kar sako) means you never harm the original photo.
You make changes in a way you can undo or turn off later.

Why does this matter? Because you will change your mind. You will want to go
back. If you edited right on the original, you cannot. If you worked safely, you
can fix anything.

Two simple habits keep you safe:

1. **Work on a copy.** Before you retouch, copy your photo layer. Edit the copy.
   The original stays clean below it.
2. **Use an adjustment layer.** An **adjustment layer** (Roman Urdu: ek alag
   parat jo color ya light badalti hai bina asal photo ko chhue) changes light or
   color on its own layer. You can turn it off or delete it any time. The photo
   underneath never changes.

!!! warning "Never edit the only copy"
    If you edit your one and only photo and save over it, the original is gone
    for good. Always copy the layer first, or keep the first file safe. Then edit
    the copy.

!!! tip "tl;dr"
    Retouching fixes small problems in a photo. The Spot Healing Brush removes
    small marks with a click. The Clone Stamp copies a clean area over a bad one.
    Dodge lightens and Burn darkens. Always work non-destructively, on copies and
    adjustment layers, so you can undo anything.
