# 3.1 Raster & Photoshop Tour

!!! info "Why this lesson?"
    By the end of this lesson you can explain what raster art is, find your way
    around the Photoshop window, use the tools panel and the layers panel, and
    save your work as a `.psd` file.

    *(Roman Urdu: Is sabaq ke baad tum bata sako ge ke raster art kya hai,
    Photoshop ki window mein ghoom sako ge, tools panel aur layers panel use kar
    sako ge, aur apna kaam `.psd` file mein save kar sako ge.)*

## What you will learn

- What raster means, and how pixels work
- How raster is different from vector
- What the tools panel is
- What the layers panel is
- What a `.psd` file is

## What is raster?

A **raster** (Roman Urdu: tasveer jo chhote chhote rangeen dabbon se banti hai)
image is made of tiny squares of color. Each tiny square is a **pixel** (Roman
Urdu: ek chhota rangeen dabba jo screen par tasveer banata hai). Put millions of
pixels together and you see a photo.

Every photo on your phone is raster. Open one and zoom in very close. You will
see little squares. Those are pixels.

A raster image has a fixed number of pixels. If you make it much bigger, the
pixels get bigger too. The photo then looks soft and blocky. This is why a small
photo looks bad when you blow it up.

!!! tip "Use Photopea if you cannot pay"
    Photoshop is paid software. It has a 7-day free trial, then it costs money.
    Good news: there is a free tool called **Photopea** (Roman Urdu: ek free
    online tool jo Photoshop jaisa kaam karta hai). It runs in your browser at
    [photopea.com](https://www.photopea.com). You install nothing. It looks and
    works almost the same as Photoshop. In this chapter we say "Photoshop or
    Photopea" because the steps are the same in both. Use whichever you have.

## Raster vs vector

There are two main kinds of digital art. You should know both.

- **Raster** (Roman Urdu: pixels se bani tasveer) is made of pixels. It is great
  for photos. It gets blurry when you make it much bigger.
- **Vector** (Roman Urdu: math lines se bani tasveer jo kabhi blurry nahi hoti)
  is made of math lines and shapes. It stays sharp at any size. It is great for
  logos and icons.

Here is the simple rule. Use raster for photos. Use vector for logos and clean
shapes. You will learn vector in the next chapter with Illustrator.

!!! example "A quick test"
    Take a logo. Make it the size of a wall poster. A vector logo stays sharp.
    A raster photo of the same logo turns soft and blocky. That is the whole
    difference in one picture.

## The tools panel

When you open Photoshop or Photopea, look at the left side. That tall strip of
small buttons is the **tools panel** (Roman Urdu: bayin taraf ka panel jisme sab
tools hote hain).

Each button is one tool. Some you will use a lot:

- The **Move tool** picks up a layer and moves it.
- The **Crop tool** cuts the photo down to a smaller frame.
- The **Brush tool** paints color.
- The **Eraser tool** rubs pixels away.

Hover your mouse over any tool to see its name. You do not need to learn them all
now. You will pick them up slowly as you work.

## The layers panel

Look at the bottom right. That box is the **layers panel** (Roman Urdu: panel jo
tumhari sab layers ki list dikhata hai).

A **layer** (Roman Urdu: ek shaffaf parat jis par tum alag se kaam karte ho) is
like a clear sheet. You can stack many sheets on top of each other. You can paint
on one sheet without touching the others.

Think of it like sheets of glass. The text sits on one sheet. The photo sits on
the sheet below. You can move or hide each sheet on its own.

In the layers panel you can:

- See every layer in a list
- Hide a layer by clicking its eye icon
- Drag a layer up or down to change its order
- Make a new empty layer

You will use the layers panel in almost every lesson. It is the heart of
Photoshop.

## The .psd file

When you save your work, save it as a **.psd file** (Roman Urdu: Photoshop ki
apni file jisme sab layers alag alag save rehti hain). A `.psd` keeps every layer
separate. So you can open it later and keep editing each part.

A `.jpg` or `.png` flattens everything into one image. The layers are gone. You
cannot pull them apart again.

!!! tip "Save two files"
    Save your working file as `.psd` so you keep your layers. When you are done
    and want to share it, also export a `.jpg` or `.png`. In Photopea you save a
    `.psd` from **File > Save as PSD**.

!!! tip "tl;dr"
    Raster art is made of pixels and is best for photos. Vector art is made of
    math lines and stays sharp at any size. The tools panel on the left holds
    your tools. The layers panel on the right holds your stacked layers. Save as
    `.psd` to keep your layers.
