# 3.4 Effects & Compositing

!!! info "Why this lesson?"
    By the end of this lesson you can mix layers with blend modes, change color
    and light with adjustment layers, and join two photos into one new picture.

    *(Roman Urdu: Is sabaq ke baad tum blend modes se layers ko mila sako ge,
    adjustment layers se color aur light badal sako ge, aur do photos ko mila kar
    ek nayi tasveer bana sako ge.)*

## What you will learn

- What a blend mode is
- A few useful blend modes
- How adjustment layers change color and light
- What compositing means
- How to combine two photos into one

## What is a blend mode?

A **blend mode** (Roman Urdu: tareeqa jo batata hai ek layer neeche wali layer ke
saath kaise mile) tells a layer how to mix with the layer below it.

Normally a layer just sits on top and covers what is under it. A blend mode
changes that. It can let the colors mix, or add light, or darken the layer below.

You set the blend mode at the top of the layers panel. There is a small drop-down
menu, usually set to "Normal". Click it to pick a different mode.

A few useful ones:

- **Multiply** makes things darker. It is good for shadows.
- **Screen** makes things lighter. It is good for light and glow.
- **Overlay** boosts contrast. It makes colors pop.

!!! tip "Just try them"
    You do not need to know the math behind blend modes. Pick a layer, open the
    drop-down, and click each mode one by one. Watch what happens. Keep the one
    that looks right. Trying is faster than reading.

## Adjustment layers

You met adjustment layers in the last lesson. Here is more.

An **adjustment layer** (Roman Urdu: ek alag parat jo color ya light badalti hai
bina asal photo ko chhue) changes the look of the layers below it. It sits on its
own layer, so you can turn it off or delete it any time.

Common adjustment layers:

- **Brightness/Contrast** makes the photo lighter, darker, or punchier.
- **Hue/Saturation** changes colors and how strong they are.
- **Levels** or **Curves** fix the dark and light balance.

Add one from the bottom of the layers panel, the button that looks like a half
black, half white circle. Because it is its own layer, your photo stays safe.

## What is compositing?

**Compositing** (Roman Urdu: do ya zyada photos ko mila kar ek nayi tasveer
banana) means joining two or more photos into one new image. A movie poster with
a hero, a city, and a sky is a composite. Each part came from a different photo.

The trick is to make all the parts look like they belong together. You match
their light, their color, and their edges.

## Combine two photos into one

Let us join two photos. Say one photo of a person, and one photo of a background.

1. Open both photos in Photoshop or Photopea.
2. Select the person with the Quick Selection tool.
3. Add a layer mask so only the person shows. The old background hides.
4. Drag the person onto the background photo. Now they are on two layers in one
   file.
5. Put the person layer above the background layer.
6. Use the Move tool to place the person where you want.

Now make it look real:

- Resize the person so the size makes sense.
- Add a Brightness/Contrast or Hue/Saturation adjustment layer to match the
  colors.
- Add a soft shadow under the person if the scene has shadows.

!!! warning "Match the light"
    The most common composite mistake is light that does not match. If the
    background is a sunset, the person cannot have bright daytime light. Use
    adjustment layers to fix the color and brightness until both parts agree.

!!! tip "tl;dr"
    A blend mode tells a layer how to mix with the layer below it, like Multiply
    to darken or Screen to lighten. Adjustment layers change color and light
    without harming the photo. Compositing joins two or more photos into one. To
    sell it, match the size, color, and light of every part.

## Optional assignment

Pick two photos. One main subject, like a person or an animal, and one
background. Cut out the subject with a selection and a mask. Place it on the
background. Add a title in big text. Use an adjustment layer to match the colors.
Make a simple movie-poster composite. Save it as a `.psd`, and export a `.jpg` to
share.
