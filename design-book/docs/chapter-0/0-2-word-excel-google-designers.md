# 0.2 Word, Excel & Google Search for Designers

!!! info "Why this lesson?"
    A designer does more than draw. You collect ideas, you send clients a price
    list, and you find images you are allowed to use. This lesson shows you how
    to do all three with free Google tools.

    *(Roman Urdu: Designer sirf design nahi banata. Tum ideas jama karte ho,
    client ko price bhejte ho, aur aisi images dhoondte ho jinhe istemaal karne
    ki ijazat ho. Yeh sabaq teenon kaam Google ke free tools se sikhata hai.)*

## What you will learn

- How to build a simple moodboard in Google Docs
- How to make a basic client invoice in Google Sheets
- How to find images you are allowed to use
- How to do a reverse image search

## A moodboard in Google Docs

A **moodboard** (Roman Urdu: ek page jis par tum apni pasand ke colors, photos
aur ideas ek jagah lagate ho) shows the look and feel of a project. You make it
before you design, so you and the client agree on the style.

Make one in Google Docs:

1. Open `docs.google.com` and click **Blank document**.
2. Type a title at the top, like "Coffee Shop Moodboard".
3. Click **Insert**, then **Image**, then **Search the web** or **Upload**.
4. Add four or five images that show the mood you want.
5. Under the images, write three or four words for the feel, like "warm, cozy,
   simple, brown".

!!! tip "Keep a moodboard small"
    Five strong images beat twenty weak ones. Pick only the images that match
    the feeling. Remove anything that does not fit.

## A client invoice in Google Sheets

An **invoice** (Roman Urdu: ek bill jo tum client ko bhejte ho jisme kaam aur
uska price likha hota hai) lists what you did and what it costs. A clean invoice
makes you look professional.

Make one in Google Sheets:

1. Open `sheets.google.com` and click **Blank spreadsheet**.
2. In row 1, type these headings: **Item** in A1, **Price (PKR)** in B1.
3. In the next rows, list your work. For example:
    - A2: "Logo design", B2: 5000
    - A3: "Three social posts", B3: 3000
    - A4: "Business card", B4: 2000
4. Click the cell where you want the total, say B5.
5. Type this and press Enter:

```
=SUM(B2:B4)
```

The cell now shows `10000`. The **`=SUM`** (Roman Urdu: ek formula jo niche
diye gaye numbers ko jor deta hai) adds the prices for you. If you change a
price, the total updates by itself.

!!! example "Why a formula beats a calculator"
    Say the client adds one more poster. You type the price in B5, change the
    formula to `=SUM(B2:B5)`, and the total fixes itself. No mistakes, no
    re-adding by hand.

## Finding images you are allowed to use

You cannot use every image you find online. Many images belong to someone else.
Using them can get you and your client in trouble.

Here is how to find images you may use:

1. Go to `images.google.com` and search for what you need, like "coffee cup".
2. Click **Tools** under the search bar.
3. Click **Usage rights**.
4. Choose **Creative Commons licenses** to see images you may reuse.

**Usage rights** (Roman Urdu: yeh batate hain ke kis image ko tum istemaal kar
sakte ho aur kis ko nahi) is the setting that filters images by permission.
Always read the rule for each image, because some still ask you to credit the
maker.

!!! warning "Free does not always mean free to use"
    An image being on Google does not mean you can use it. Check the usage
    rights first. For safe free images, sites like Unsplash and Pexels are good
    starting points.

## Reverse image search

A **reverse image search** (Roman Urdu: image se search karna, jisme tum lafz
nahi, ek tasveer dete ho) lets you search using a picture instead of words. It
helps you find where an image came from, or find bigger versions.

1. Go to `images.google.com`.
2. Click the small camera icon in the search bar.
3. Upload an image or paste an image link.
4. Google shows you matching and similar images.

This is handy when a client sends you a tiny logo and you need a bigger one, or
when you want to check if an image is really free.

!!! tip "tl;dr"
    A moodboard in Google Docs sets the style before you design. An invoice in
    Google Sheets with `=SUM` adds your prices for you. Use Google Images Tools,
    then Usage rights, to find images you are allowed to use. Reverse image
    search lets you search with a picture.
