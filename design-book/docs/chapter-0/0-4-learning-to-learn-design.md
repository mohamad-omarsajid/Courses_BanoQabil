# 0.4 Learning to Learn (designer edition)

!!! info "Why this lesson?"
    Good designers are not born. They build their eye over time by looking,
    copying, and asking for feedback. This lesson gives you simple habits that
    make you better month after month.

    *(Roman Urdu: Achhe designer paida nahi hote. Woh dekh kar, copy kar ke aur
    raye le kar apni nazar tez karte hain. Yeh sabaq tumhe aise aasaan tareeqe
    deta hai jo tumhe behtar banate rehte hain.)*

## What you will learn

- How to keep a visual diary
- The redraw a screenshot exercise
- How to study real designs
- How to ask for feedback well

## Keep a visual diary

A **visual diary** (Roman Urdu: ek folder ya copy jisme tum apni pasand ke
designs jama karte ho aur likhte ho ke woh kyun achhe lage) is a collection of
designs you love. The key part is the "why".

Here is how to do it:

1. When you see a design you like, save a screenshot to a folder.
2. Next to it, write one line on why you like it. For example, "The colors are
   calm and the text is easy to read."
3. Look back at it sometimes. You will start to see patterns in what you like.

Saving designs is easy. Writing the why is what trains your eye. Do not skip it.

!!! tip "Use your inspiration folder"
    Remember the `inspiration` folder you made in lesson 0.1? That is your
    visual diary. Drop your saved designs there and keep a notes file beside
    them.

## The redraw a screenshot exercise

The fastest way to learn is to copy a real design by hand. This is the **redraw
exercise** (Roman Urdu: ek asli app ya website ki screen ko khud dobara banana,
seekhne ke liye).

Try it:

1. Pick a simple screen, like a login page from a phone app.
2. Take a screenshot of it.
3. Open your design tool and try to rebuild it from scratch.
4. Match the colors, the spacing, and the font sizes as best you can.

You are not stealing. You are practising, like a music student copying a song.
You will notice tiny choices you never saw before, like how much space sits
around a button.

!!! warning "Redraw to learn, not to sell"
    Copying a real design is great practice. But do not sell a copy as your own
    work, and do not pass it to a client. Learn from it, then make your own.

## Study real designs

Look closely at designs you use every day. Open an app you like and ask
yourself simple questions:

- What is the biggest thing on the screen, and why?
- How many colors do they use?
- How much empty space is there?
- Where do your eyes go first?

The more you ask these questions, the more you notice. Soon you will see good
and bad design everywhere.

## Ask for feedback well

Feedback makes you better, but only if you ask the right way. A vague question
gets a vague answer.

- Do not ask "Is this good?" You will only get "yeah, nice".
- Ask instead, "Is the heading easy to read? Does the color feel calm or
  loud?"
- Tell people who it is for. "This poster is for a kids party."
- Listen without arguing. Say thank you, then decide what to change.

!!! example "A better feedback question"
    "This is a poster for a small bakery. Can you read the shop name from far
    away? Does the brown and pink feel warm or muddy?"

Specific questions get specific, useful answers.

!!! tip "tl;dr"
    Keep a visual diary and always write why you like a design. Redraw real
    screens to train your eye. Study designs you use daily by asking simple
    questions. Ask for feedback with specific questions, not "is this good?"

## Knowledge check

1. What should you always write next to a design in your visual diary?
2. Why does redrawing a real design help you learn?
3. Name one good question to ask while studying a design.
4. Why is "Is this good?" a weak feedback question?
