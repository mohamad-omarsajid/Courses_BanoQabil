# 1.3 Typography

!!! info "Why this lesson?"
    By the end of this lesson you can tell font types apart. You can also pair
    two fonts and set spacing so your text looks clean and reads easily.

    *(Roman Urdu: Is sabaq ke baad tum font ki kismon ko pehchan sako ge. Aur
    tum do fonts ko saath laga sako ge aur spacing aise rakho ge ke text saaf
    aur parhne mein aasaan lage.)*

## What you will learn

- The three main font families: serif, sans-serif, and mono
- How to pair two fonts
- Kerning, the space between letters
- Leading, the space between lines
- Line length, how wide a line of text should be

## What is typography?

**Typography** (Roman Urdu: harf aur text ko khoobsoorat aur parhne mein aasaan
banane ka fun) is the art of arranging text. It covers which fonts you use and
how you space them. Good typography makes reading feel easy and calm.

## Three font families

A **font** (Roman Urdu: harf likhne ka andaaz ya style) has a style. Most fonts
fall into three families.

### Serif

**Serif** (Roman Urdu: woh fonts jin ke haron ke kinaron par chhote pair hote
hain) fonts have small feet at the ends of letters. They feel classic and
formal. Times New Roman is a serif font. Books and newspapers use them a lot.

### Sans-serif

**Sans-serif** (Roman Urdu: bina pairon wale saaf fonts) fonts have no feet.
The edges are clean and plain. Arial is a sans-serif font. Apps and websites
use them a lot because they look modern and read well on screens.

### Monospace

**Monospace** (Roman Urdu: woh fonts jin mein har harf barabar jagah leta hai),
or mono, gives every letter the same width. Code editors use them. A wide `m`
and a thin `i` take the same space, so columns line up.

!!! example "Where each family fits"
    - Serif: a wedding card or a printed book
    - Sans-serif: a phone app or a website
    - Mono: code, receipts, or anything in neat columns

## Pairing two fonts

Most designs use just two fonts. One for headings, one for body text. This is
**font pairing** (Roman Urdu: do fonts ko saath chunna jo achay lagein).

A simple safe rule helps you start.

1. Pick one font for headings. A bold sans-serif works well.
2. Pick one easy font for body text. A clean serif or sans-serif works well.
3. Make sure the two look different but not like they are fighting.

!!! tip "Two fonts is plenty"
    New designers often use five fonts on one page. It looks messy. Stick to
    two. You can change size and weight to add variety instead.

!!! warning "Avoid lookalike pairs"
    Do not pair two fonts that look almost the same. The viewer feels something
    is off but cannot say what. Pick fonts with a clear, on purpose difference.

## Kerning

**Kerning** (Roman Urdu: do haron ke beech ki jagah) is the space between two
single letters. Bad kerning makes letters crash together or drift apart. Most
of the time the font handles this for you. You only fix it in big headings and
logos, where gaps show.

## Leading

**Leading** (Roman Urdu: do lines ke beech ki jagah, upar neeche) is the space
between lines of text. It is also called line spacing.

- Lines too close together feel cramped and tiring.
- Lines with a little room feel calm and easy to read.

For body text, give your lines a bit of breathing room. It makes a big
difference in how easy your text feels.

## Line length

**Line length** (Roman Urdu: ek line mein kitne harf ya alfaaz hon) is how wide
a line of text is. Very long lines tire the eye. Very short lines break the
flow.

A good range is about 50 to 75 characters per line. Roughly 9 to 12 words. If
your text runs edge to edge on a wide screen, make the column narrower.

!!! tip "Play these free type games"
    Your eye for spacing gets sharp with practice. Try these two free games:

    - [Kern Type](https://type.method.ac), space the letters by feel
    - [Shape Type](https://shape.method.ac), shape the letters themselves

!!! tip "tl;dr"
    Fonts fall into three families: serif with feet, sans-serif without feet,
    and mono with equal widths. Use two fonts, one for headings and one for
    body. Kerning is space between letters. Leading is space between lines.
    Keep lines about 50 to 75 characters long for easy reading.

## Optional assignment

Pick two fonts that go well together, one for headings and one for body text.
Use them to design a one-page poster for any topic you like, for example a book
club or a cricket match.

Set your leading so the lines have room to breathe. Keep your body lines from
running too wide. Save it and look at it on a phone to check it reads well.
