# 1.2 Color Theory

!!! info "Why this lesson?"
    By the end of this lesson you can write colors the way computers do. You can
    also pick colors that look good together and stay easy to read.

    *(Roman Urdu: Is sabaq ke baad tum colors ko computer ke tareeqe se likh
    sako ge. Aur tum aise colors chun sako ge jo saath achay lagein aur parhne
    mein aasaan rahein.)*

## What you will learn

- How computers store color with RGB
- How to write a color as a HEX code
- The color wheel
- Complementary and analogous colors
- Why contrast keeps text readable

## How computers see color: RGB

Screens make color by mixing three lights: red, green, and blue. This is
**RGB** (Roman Urdu: red, green, aur blue light ko mila kar color banana). Each
light has a value from 0 to 255. 0 means off. 255 means full.

- Red 255, Green 0, Blue 0 makes pure red.
- Red 0, Green 0, Blue 0 makes black, all lights off.
- Red 255, Green 255, Blue 255 makes white, all lights full.

## Writing color as HEX

Typing three numbers is slow. So designers use a shorter code called **HEX**
(Roman Urdu: color ko likhne ka chhota code jo # se shuru hota hai). A HEX code
starts with a `#` and has six characters.

The six characters are three pairs. The first pair is red, the next is green,
the last is blue.

```text
#0fab95
```

In `#0fab95`, `0f` is red, `ab` is green, and `95` is blue. You do not need to
do the math by hand. Design tools give you the code when you pick a color.

!!! example "A few HEX colors to know"
    - `#000000` is black
    - `#ffffff` is white
    - `#ff0000` is red
    - `#0fab95` is a teal green

!!! tip "Copy and paste HEX"
    When a color looks good in one tool, copy its HEX code. Paste it into your
    next design. This keeps your colors exactly the same everywhere.

## The color wheel

A **color wheel** (Roman Urdu: ek gol shakl jis par saare colors tarteeb se
lage hote hain) is a circle of colors in order, like a rainbow bent into a
ring. It helps you see which colors sit near each other and which sit across.

You do not need to memorize it. You just need to know two simple ways to pick
colors from it.

## Complementary colors

**Complementary colors** (Roman Urdu: woh do colors jo wheel par ek dusre ke
bilkul saamne hote hain) are two colors opposite each other on the wheel. For
example, blue and orange, or red and green.

They create strong contrast. Use them when you want one thing to pop, like a
bright button on a calm background. Do not fill a whole design with both, or it
gets loud.

## Analogous colors

**Analogous colors** (Roman Urdu: woh colors jo wheel par ek dusre ke pass pass
hote hain) sit next to each other on the wheel. For example, blue, blue green,
and green.

They feel calm and friendly because they are close family. Use them when you
want a soft, smooth look that does not fight for attention.

## Contrast and readability

**Contrast** (Roman Urdu: do colors ke beech ka farq, jaise halka aur gehra)
is the difference between two colors. Text needs good contrast against its
background, or people cannot read it.

- Dark text on a light background reads well.
- Light text on a dark background reads well.
- Light gray text on a white background is hard to read.

!!! warning "Test your text"
    Pretty colors mean nothing if the words vanish. Always check that your text
    is easy to read. Show it to a friend on a phone screen if you can.

!!! tip "Play these free color games"
    Color sense grows fast when you play with it. Try these two free games:

    - [Hex Invaders](https://hexinvaders.com), match HEX codes to colors
    - [Color by method.ac](https://color.method.ac), train your eye for color

!!! tip "tl;dr"
    Screens mix red, green, and blue to make color, called RGB. HEX is the
    short `#` code for a color, like `#0fab95`. The color wheel shows colors in
    order. Complementary colors sit across and pop. Analogous colors sit close
    and feel calm. Good contrast keeps text readable.

## Knowledge check

1. What three lights make color on a screen?
2. In `#0fab95`, which pair is the green value?
3. What are complementary colors?
4. What are analogous colors?
5. Why does text need good contrast?

    Screen red, green, aur blue light mila kar color banati hai, isse RGB kehte
    hain. HEX color ka chhota code hai jo # se shuru hota hai, jaise `#0fab95`.
    Complementary colors wheel par ek dusre ke saamne hote hain aur namayan
    lagte hain. Analogous colors pass pass hote hain aur narm lagte hain. Achi

## Optional assignment

Make a palette of five colors. Pick one main color, then use complementary or
analogous colors to choose the rest. Write down all five HEX codes.

Now use that same palette in two simple designs, for example a quote card and a
small poster. Keep the colors the same in both. Notice how a fixed palette
makes your work feel finished.
