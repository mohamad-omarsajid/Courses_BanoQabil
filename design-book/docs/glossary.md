# Glossary

Every new word from the lessons, in simple English and Roman Urdu, with one
example. This page grows as the course grows.

### Bleed
Extra space at the edge of a print design that gets cut off, so the color
reaches the very edge.
Roman Urdu: print design ke kinare par thori extra jagah jo cut ho jati hai.
Example: A poster with bleed has no white edge after cutting.

### Brand kit
A saved set of your colors, fonts, and logos in a tool like Canva.
Roman Urdu: tumhare colors, fonts aur logos ka saved set.
Example: Your brand kit keeps every post looking the same.

### DPI
Dots per inch. How many dots of ink fit in one inch. Higher DPI looks sharper in print.
Roman Urdu: ek inch mein kitne dots aate hain. Zyada DPI print mein saaf dikhta hai.
Example: Print designs use 300 DPI.

### Hierarchy
The order that guides the eye, so the most important thing is seen first.
Roman Urdu: woh tarteeb jo aankh ko rasta dikhati hai, sab se zaroori cheez pehle.
Example: A big bold title has more hierarchy than small text.

### Raster
An image made of tiny squares called pixels. It blurs when you zoom in too much.
Roman Urdu: pixels se bani image jo zyada zoom karne par dhundli ho jati hai.
Example: A photo is a raster image.

### Vector
Art made of math lines and shapes. It stays sharp at any size.
Roman Urdu: math lines aur shapes se bani art jo har size par saaf rehti hai.
Example: A logo should be a vector so it never blurs.

### Wireframe
A simple, plain layout of a screen, without color or final art.
Roman Urdu: screen ka saada khaka, bina color aur final art ke.
Example: You wireframe an app before you design it nicely.
