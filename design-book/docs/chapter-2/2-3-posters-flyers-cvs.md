# 2.3 Posters, Flyers & CVs

!!! info "Why this lesson?"
    By the end of this lesson you can make a design that is ready to print,
    understand bleed and DPI, and export a print PDF, including your own CV.

    *(Roman Urdu: Is sabaq ke baad tum aisa design bana sako ge jo print ke liye
    tayar ho, bleed aur DPI samajh sako ge, aur ek print PDF export kar sako ge,
    apne CV samet.)*

## What you will learn

- What print-ready means
- What bleed is
- What DPI is, and why 300 DPI is for print
- How to export a print PDF
- How to make a simple CV

## Print-ready design

A design for the screen and a design for print are not the same. When you print
on paper, the printer needs a cleaner, higher quality file.

A **print-ready** (Roman Urdu: aisa design jo print press par theek chhapne ke
liye tayar ho) design is set up so it prints sharp, with no white edges and no
blurry parts. Posters, flyers, and CVs are all printed, so they should be
print-ready.

## What is bleed?

**Bleed** (Roman Urdu: design ke kinaron par thori extra jagah jo katne ke baad
hat jati hai) is a small extra strip of design past the edge of the page.

Why it exists:

- Printers cut paper in big stacks, and the cut is never perfect.
- If your color stops exactly at the edge, a tiny white line can show after the cut.
- Bleed makes the color go a little past the edge, so after the cut there is no
  white line.

In Canva, you turn this on. Open the **File** menu, find **Settings** or **View
settings**, and tick **Show print bleed**. Stretch your background to fill the
bleed area too.

!!! warning "White edges look cheap"
    A thin white line on the edge of a printed flyer looks like a mistake. Turn on
    bleed and let your background run off the edge. This small step makes printed
    work look clean.

## What is DPI?

**DPI** (Roman Urdu: ek inch mein kitne dots, yaani print kitna saaf hoga) stands
for dots per inch. It tells you how many tiny dots of ink fit in one inch of
paper. More dots means a sharper print.

Here is the rule:

- For print, use `300` DPI. This is sharp and clean on paper.
- For screen only, like a social post, `72` DPI is fine.

So `300 DPI` means 300 dots packed into every inch. That is enough detail that
your eye sees smooth lines, not blocky pixels. Always aim for 300 DPI on anything
you will print.

!!! tip "Canva handles DPI for you"
    When you export a print PDF in Canva, it sets a high quality for you. You do
    not type 300 yourself. Just choose the print PDF option, which you will see
    next.

## Export a print PDF

A **PDF** (Roman Urdu: ek file jo har computer aur har printer par same dikhti
hai) is the file type printers want. It keeps your design exact.

To export:

1. Click **Share** at the top right.
2. Click **Download**.
3. For File type, choose **PDF Print**.
4. Tick **Crop marks and bleed** if you are sending it to a print shop.
5. Click **Download**.

Use **PDF Print** for anything going on paper. Use **PDF Standard** or PNG only
for screen.

## Make a simple CV

A CV is a one page list of who you are and what you can do. Canva has many CV
templates, so you do not start from blank.

A clear CV has:

1. Your name and how to reach you, at the top.
2. A short line about yourself, two sentences.
3. Your skills, as a short list.
4. Your education.
5. Any work or projects you have done.

!!! example "Keep it simple"
    Pick a clean CV template with one or two colors. Do not use many fonts or
    bright backgrounds. A calm, easy to read CV looks more professional than a
    busy one.

!!! tip "tl;dr"
    Print needs more care than screen. Bleed is extra design past the edge so the
    cut leaves no white line. DPI is dots per inch, and 300 DPI is the sharp setting
    for print. Export with the PDF Print option. A clean CV uses a simple template
    and few colors.
