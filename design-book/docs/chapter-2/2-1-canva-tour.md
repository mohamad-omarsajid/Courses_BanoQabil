# 2.1 Canva Tour

!!! info "Why this lesson?"
    By the end of this lesson you can sign up for Canva for free, find your way
    around it, open a template, and make your first social media post.

    *(Roman Urdu: Is sabaq ke baad tum Canva par free account bana sako ge, isme
    raasta dhoond sako ge, ek template khol sako ge, aur apni pehli social media
    post bana sako ge.)*

## What you will learn

- How to sign up for Canva for free
- What the dashboard is
- How to search for templates
- The editor: elements, text, and uploads
- What a brand kit is

## Sign up for free

Canva runs in your browser. You do not download anything.

1. Open your browser and go to `canva.com`.
2. Click **Sign up**.
3. You can sign up with a Google account, or with your email.
4. Pick **Student** or **Personal** when it asks why you are joining.

The free plan is plenty for this whole course. You get thousands of templates,
photos, and fonts for free. You do not need to pay.

!!! tip "Use the free plan"
    Canva will show you a paid plan called Canva Pro. You do not need it. Skip
    any free trial offer. Everything in this chapter works on the free plan.

## The dashboard

The **dashboard** (Roman Urdu: pehla page jo Canva kholne par dikhta hai) is your
home screen. It is the first thing you see after you log in.

On the dashboard you can:

- Start a new design with a big purple button.
- Search for a design type, like "Instagram post".
- See your past designs so you can open them again.

Your work saves on its own. You do not need a save button. Canva keeps every
design in your account.

## Search for templates

A **template** (Roman Urdu: pehle se bana design jise tum apne hisaab se badal
sakte ho) is a ready design made by someone else. You change the words, colors,
and photos to make it yours.

To find one:

1. Type what you want in the search box at the top, like "birthday post".
2. Press Enter.
3. Click a template you like.
4. Canva opens it in the editor, ready for you to change.

Templates save you a lot of time. You do not start from a blank page. You start
from something that already looks good.

## The editor

The **editor** (Roman Urdu: woh screen jahan tum design banate aur badalte ho) is
where you do the real work. Your design sits in the middle. The tools sit on the
left.

The three tools you will use most are:

1. **Elements** (Roman Urdu: shapes, lines, photos, aur icons jo tum design mein
   daal sakte ho). Click Elements to add shapes, lines, photos, and icons.
2. **Text** (Roman Urdu: likhne ke liye). Click Text to add a heading or a
   sentence. You can change the font, size, and color.
3. **Uploads** (Roman Urdu: apni photos ya logo Canva mein laana). Click Uploads
   to bring in your own photos and your logo from your phone or computer.

!!! example "Change one word"
    Open any template. Double click on a piece of text. The words turn blue,
    which means you can type. Delete the old words and type your own. That is the
    main thing you do in Canva, again and again.

## The brand kit

A **brand kit** (Roman Urdu: tumhare saved colors, fonts, aur logo ek jagah)
holds the look you want to reuse. It saves your colors, your fonts, and your logo
in one place.

Why this helps:

- You pick the same colors every time, so your designs match.
- You do not hunt for the right hex color again and again.
- Your logo is one click away.

You set it up once. Then every new design can use the same colors and fonts. This
keeps all your work looking like it came from one person.

!!! tip "Save your colors"
    On the free plan you can save a small set of colors. Pick two or three you
    like, for example a main color and a soft background color. Write down the
    hex codes too, like `#1e88e5`, so you remember them.

!!! tip "tl;dr"
    Canva is free and runs in your browser. The dashboard is your home screen.
    Templates are ready designs you change to make your own. The editor holds
    Elements, Text, and Uploads. A brand kit saves your colors, fonts, and logo
    so your work matches.
