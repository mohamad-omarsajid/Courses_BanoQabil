# Chapter 0: Getting Ready

<p class="byline" markdown>Muhammad Umar Sajid · Trainer at Bano Qabil Sahiwal · Front End Development and Graphic Design</p>

Before we write any code, let us get comfortable with the computer, the internet,
and a few habits that make learning easier.

If you have used a phone but barely touched a laptop, this chapter is for you.
Nothing here is hard. Take it slow.

!!! tip "No rush"
    There is no clock and no deadline. Finish a lesson, take a break, and come
    back when you are ready. The lessons will wait for you.

## Mega assignment

!!! bq-assignment "Set up your developer identity and learning system"
    Before you write a single line of code, real developers set up the accounts
    and habits they will use for years. Do it now, properly, once.

    **What you build**

    - A professional email and a GitHub account that share the **same real name** and a clean username (something like `firstname-lastname`, not `xXgamer786Xx`), with a real photo and a one-line bio.
    - A learning journal: a Google Doc or Sheet where, after every lesson, you write the date, one line on what you learned, and one thing that confused you.
    - A weekly study plan. Self-paced does not mean no plan. Pick your days and times and write them down where you will see them.

    **Done when**

    - [ ] Your GitHub username looks like something you would put on a CV.
    - [ ] Your GitHub and email use the same real name.
    - [ ] Your learning journal has its first entry, dated today.
    - [ ] Your weekly study plan is written down somewhere visible.

    **Stretch goal:** Write a short "why I am learning this" paragraph at the top of your journal. On the hard days, you will read it.
