<p class="byline" markdown>Muhammad Umar Sajid · Trainer at Bano Qabil Sahiwal · Front End Development and Graphic Design</p>

You can build real, fast, production-ready websites now. This final chapter turns
that skill into a career. You will build a portfolio that gets you hired, polish
your projects so they shine, and learn how to find your first paying clients.

This is the bridge from learning to earning. Take it seriously. A good portfolio
and a clear plan are what turn months of study into a real income, the halal way.

## Mega assignment

!!! bq-assignment "Ship your portfolio and go after real work"
    This is the assignment the whole course was building toward. Turn your study
    into something an employer or a client can hire you from, then actually reach
    out. Done and sent beats perfect and hidden.

    **What you build and do**

    - A deployed **portfolio site** with at least three real projects: the client site you have grown since Chapter 1, the ecommerce store, and one more of your choice.
    - For each project: a live link, the source on GitHub, a clear README with screenshots, and one or two lines on the problem it solves and your role.
    - A short **about** section, a way to contact you, and your CV linked.
    - A real outreach push: send your portfolio to **five** actual opportunities (the business owner you have been building for, a job listing, an Upwork or Fiverr profile, a LinkedIn post, a local company).

    **Done when**

    - [ ] The portfolio is live on a public URL and works on mobile.
    - [ ] All three projects load, with working links and clean READMEs.
    - [ ] Someone who has never met you can understand what you build in under a minute.
    - [ ] You have sent it to at least five real people or listings, and logged the responses in your journal.

    **Stretch goal:** Record a 60-second Loom walking through your best project, and turn the client site you built into a real, paid first job.
