# Chapter 16: More animation, Framer, Anime, and beyond

<p class="byline" markdown>Muhammad Umar Sajid · Trainer at Bano Qabil Sahiwal · Front End Development and Graphic Design</p>

You learned GSAP in Chapter 12. It is powerful, but it is not the only tool. This
chapter shows you two more animation libraries, goes deeper on smooth scroll, and
teaches the most useful skill of all: knowing which tool to reach for and when to
hold back.

By the end you can pick the right library for a job, animate React the easy way
with Framer Motion, and keep your motion fast and kind to every user.

## Lessons in this chapter

- [ ] [16.1 Framer Motion](16-1-framer-motion.md): the animation library made for React.
- [ ] [16.2 Anime.js](16-2-anime-js.md): a small, fun library, and how it compares to GSAP.
- [ ] [16.3 Lenis deep dive](16-3-lenis-deep.md): smooth scroll options and the wider scroll family.
- [ ] [16.4 Choosing the right tool](16-4-choosing-the-right-tool.md): when to use what, and when not to animate at all.

!!! tip "More tools, same rule"
    Each library here does similar work in a different style. Learn one well, then
    the others feel familiar. The hard part is taste, not syntax.

## Mega assignment

!!! bq-assignment "Add Framer Motion polish to your store"
    The store works. Now make it feel finished with the small, fast interactions
    that good apps have and beginners forget.

    **What you build**

    - **Page transitions** between routes with Framer Motion, so navigation feels smooth instead of a hard cut.
    - **Micro-interactions**: buttons that respond to tap, cards that lift on hover, a cart badge that bounces when an item is added.
    - An **animated cart drawer or modal** that slides in and out cleanly.
    - A written one-paragraph decision: for this project, when would you reach for Framer Motion versus GSAP, and why.

    **Done when**

    - [ ] Moving between pages animates smoothly with no flash.
    - [ ] At least three micro-interactions respond to the user, and none feel slow.
    - [ ] The cart drawer or a modal opens and closes with motion, and traps focus correctly.
    - [ ] Reduced-motion users still get a fully usable, calm experience.

    **Stretch goal:** Add a shared-layout animation (an element that smoothly morphs from a card into the product detail view).
